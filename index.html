Privacy Policy

Last updated: May 26, 2026

Short version: Budget collects nothing and has no servers. Everything you enter stays on your iPhone — and, only if you turn on iCloud sync, in your own private iCloud. No accounts, no analytics, no ads, no tracking.

The data you enter stays on your device

Budget stores your transactions, categories, budgets, and recurring rules locally on your iPhone using Apple's on-device storage. This information is never transmitted to us or to anyone else, because Budget has no servers and makes no network connections to send your data anywhere. The only exception is the optional iCloud sync described below, which uses your own private iCloud account — still never our servers.

We don't collect or track anything

  • No account or sign-in.
  • No analytics, telemetry, or usage tracking.
  • No advertising and no advertising identifiers.
  • No third-party SDKs that collect data.
  • No personal information is gathered, sold, or shared — there is none to share.

iCloud sync (optional)

Budget can optionally keep your data in sync across your own devices using iCloud. This is off by default — you turn it on in Settings ▸ iCloud. When it's on, your transactions, categories, budgets, and recurring rules are stored in your private iCloud account (operated by Apple) so they appear on your other devices and are backed up. That data lives in your personal iCloud; it is never sent to us, and we have no servers and no way to see it. Apple's handling of iCloud data is governed by Apple's Privacy Policy. You can turn sync off at any time, and remove the data by erasing it in the app or deleting it from iCloud.

Importing a statement

If you import a PDF or photo of a statement, it is read and analyzed entirely on your iPhone using Apple's on-device technologies. The file and its contents are never uploaded. You review and approve the transactions before anything is added.

Exporting and sharing

When you choose to export your data as a CSV file, it is created on your device and shared only where you direct it (for example, to Files, Mail, or another app you pick). What happens to it after that is governed by the app or service you share it with.

Face ID / Touch ID

If you enable the optional app lock, authentication is handled by iOS. Budget never sees or stores your biometric data — it only receives a yes/no result from the system.

Deleting your data

You can erase everything at any time from Settings ▸ Erase all data inside the app, or by deleting the app, which removes all of its data from your device.

Children's privacy

Budget does not collect any data from anyone, including children, and is safe for users of all ages.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted on this page with a new "last updated" date.
