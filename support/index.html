Help & Support

Budget is a private, on-device tracker that organizes your money around your own pay cycle.

Need a hand or have feedback? Email crose2016@gmail.com — we read every message.

How do budget periods work?

Set the day your period starts in Settings ▸ Period starts on the… (for example, the 21st, when you get paid). Every total, chart, and budget then lines up with that cycle instead of the calendar month. Changing the day just regroups your existing transactions — it never moves or duplicates them.

A new period begins on its own: when the start day arrives, the app simply starts grouping new transactions into the new period and your recurring items fill in. Nothing is created or reset — last period is always a tap back with the arrows on the Period screen.

Can I see upcoming periods?

Yes. Tap the › arrow on the Period or Budgets screen to look ahead a few periods. Future periods show a forecast: the income and expenses your recurring items will add, and how that stacks up against the budget that will be in force. These are projections — nothing is actually logged until the period arrives.

How do budgets work?

On the Budgets tab, tap Edit to set an overall monthly limit and per-category limits. Changes apply from the current period forward — adjusting a budget never rewrites how earlier periods were judged. Turn the whole feature off in Settings if you'd rather not track budgets.

How do I set up recurring income or expenses?

Two ways. The quickest: when you log a transaction, flip on Make recurring and pick Monthly or Bi-weekly — that entry becomes the first occurrence and the rest fill in automatically. Or manage them on their own under Settings ▸ Recurring. Rent, subscriptions, and paychecks then appear in each new period without re-entering them, and show up in the forecast for upcoming periods.

Why does my biweekly paycheck show up 2–3 times some months?

Biweekly means every two weeks, so most periods contain two paydays and some contain three. Budget places each one on its real date rather than a fixed day of the month.

How do I import a bank or card statement?

Tap + and choose Import from PDF or photo, or use Settings ▸ Import from statement. The file is read entirely on your iPhone and you review every transaction before it's added. (On-device extraction needs a compatible iPhone; importing a CSV works on any device.)

How do I back up or move my data?

Use Settings ▸ Export transactions to save a CSV you can keep or open in a spreadsheet, and Import from CSV… to bring it back. Importing adds rows and skips any already present, so restoring is safe.

How do I back up or sync with iCloud?

Turn on Settings ▸ iCloud ▸ Sync with iCloud, then reopen Budget. Your transactions, categories, budgets, and recurring rules are kept in your own private iCloud, so they're backed up and stay in sync across your devices signed into the same Apple Account. It's off by default — when off, everything stays only on this iPhone. Nothing is ever shared with us or anyone else. To move to a new iPhone: install Budget, sign into the same iCloud, and turn sync on.

How do I add a widget?

Home Screen: touch and hold an empty area, tap + in the top corner, search for Budget, and add the size you like — the medium and large sizes show a spending donut with your top categories, and a + on the large size jumps straight to logging an expense.

Lock Screen & StandBy: touch and hold the Lock Screen, tap Customize ▸ Lock Screen, choose a widget slot, and pick Budget. The same widgets appear in StandBy when your iPhone is charging on its side. Each shows the current period's spending, what's left, and whether you're on pace for the period.

Tap any widget to open Budget to the current period.

How do I delete my data?

Use Settings ▸ Erase all data to wipe everything and reset to the default categories, or delete the app to remove all of its data from your device.

Is my data private?

Yes. Budget has no account and no servers, and collects nothing. Your data stays on your iPhone — and, only if you turn on iCloud sync, in your own private iCloud that only you can see. See the Privacy Policy for details.
